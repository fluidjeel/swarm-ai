Quantitative Strategy & Risk Mandate: A2A Trading Engine v4.1

1. Executive Summary & Core Hypothesis

The Financial Problem: Traditional retail quantitative systems rely on static, deterministic technical indicators (e.g., Moving Averages, RSI). These systems exhibit severe "Strategy Decay" because they lack the contextual awareness to adapt to macroeconomic regime shifts. Conversely, fully autonomous LLM-based systems fail due to unacceptable latency, slippage, and an inability to perform rigid mathematical risk sizing.

The A2A Hypothesis: Alpha in the Indian derivatives market (NIFTY 50 / NIFTY BANK) is generated by tracking institutional liquidity, not retail price action. By isolating strategy execution into a zero-latency, deterministic Python core, and elevating the LLMs to an asynchronous "Parameter Tuning Layer," the system captures the adaptability of AI without the intraday risk of hallucination or slippage.

The 2026 Regulatory Context: Operating within SEBI's updated algorithmic trading framework, the system entirely avoids "Black-Box" classification. Execution logic is strictly white-box Python math; the AI merely acts as an asynchronous parameter-tuner outside of market hours, keeping execution well below the 10 Orders Per Second (OPS) retail limit while utilizing a mandated static AWS Elastic IP.

3. The Alpha Engine: Institutional Feature Pipeline

The system computes a high-density, institutional-grade data payload every 5 minutes. The Python execution engine only makes decisions based on these normalized metrics.

Market Breadth (NIFTY 500 A/D Ratio): Tracks the Advance/Decline ratio of the broader market, not just the index 50. If the NIFTY is rising but the A/D ratio is < 1.0, the system flags a heavy-weight distortion (fakeout) and blocks trend-following longs.

Volatility Target Divergence (VIX vs. ATR): Compares the 14-period Average True Range (ATR) against the India VIX. Expanding ATR with falling VIX signifies a grinding trend; contracting ATR with stable VIX signifies ideal conditions for premium selling.

Expiry-Weighted PCR Momentum: Open Interest (OI) changes are meaningless without context. The system calculates the velocity of the Put-Call Ratio over a 2-hour rolling window. Crucially, this momentum is weighted by Days to Expiry (DTE).

Overnight Institutional Priming: Between 8:00 AM and 8:45 AM, Agent 0 (LLM) analyzes US market closes, SGX/GIFT Nifty futures, and provisional FII/DII cash flow data to establish a directional macro bias before the opening bell.

4. Uncompromising Risk Guardrails

Risk constraints are hard-coded into the Python Risk Gatekeeper. LLMs have zero authority to bypass these guardrails.

4.1 Entry Constraints

Session Circuit Breaker: If realized intraday PnL hits -₹8,000 (approx. 1.1% of total capital), the system enters a HALT state for the remainder of the session. No new entries are permitted.

Undefined Risk Block: The system structurally hard-rejects any naked short options.

Stale Quote Abort: Execution is aborted if the underlying Nifty price shifts by > 10 points between feature generation and Fyers order placement.

Dynamic Sizing Scale: Base sizing is locked at 1 lot. The system only scales up via verified realized profits: Allowed Lots = 1 + floor(max(0, Realized_Capital - 600000) / 400000).

4.2 Exit Mechanisms

Retail traders bleed capital on discretionary exits. The A2A system utilizes purely mathematical exit vectors that are instantly deployed to the exchange server.

Hard Stop (Broker Level): A strict 2.5x ATR Cover/Bracket order is placed instantly at execution to protect against EC2 power outages or kernel panics.

Soft Stop (Memory Level): Directional trades are governed by a dynamic trailing stop. Range trades (Credit Spreads) execute a hard take-profit at 60% theta capture relative to maximum collectable premium.
